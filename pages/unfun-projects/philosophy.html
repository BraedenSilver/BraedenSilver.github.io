Website Philosophy

I’ve redesigned my personal website more times than I can count, each version a little better than the last. But it wasn’t until I started browsing the personal pages of professors, especially those at top schools that I realized I’d been looking at it all wrong

Raw HTML and JavaScript. Barely any CSS, no animations, no frameworks. Just text and links.

That’s it. Most professors don’t bother with design. Their sites are stripped down to the essentials: a bio, research links, and maybe a CV. It’s always readable, loads instantly, and doesn’t break when trends change.

That’s why my website is bare-bones now. No fancy colors, no pictures, no animation. Just text and links to the things that matter.
